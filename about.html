Yelyzaveta Ziborova

Throughout my life drawing was the one thing which I could not imagine living without. I used to always doodle on scraps of paper as a kid, drawing different characters from my favorite cartoons. This passion for drawing stayed with me throughout the time, although, at some points my relationship with drawing was complicated, I always returned to it in the end. Drawing is the medium which helps me to convey myself and my feelings best.


Although my works are not limited to digital or traditional illustrations, I often explore other mediums like animation, interactive installation or sculpture. When doing so, I always try to bring in the drawing aspect into my projects.


In my works and illustrations I tend to focus a lot on characters, their designs or their stories. Characters that are featured in my drawings are usually my own characters, who in some sense are self-referential and whom I always give something from me – be it a personality trait or a piece of clothing I own. At the same time, being really into fandoms and modern popular culture, I do a lot of fanart illustrations which feature characters from an already existing media.


Apart from the character aspect, I put heavy emphasis on the fashion aspect in my illustrations. Taking inspiration from the 1960-1970’s fashion, illustrations and posters, my style is heavily inspired by the period. Vivid colors, bold shapes, and exaggerated poses are some of the highlights of my drawing style.


Material-wise, I am mostly a digital artist and in my works I like to imitate the traditional feeling and materials with digital tools. However, I am not limiting myself only to digital tools, and I often create traditional illustrations and prints. Texture, materials and the tactility experience are other important parts in my works to which I pay a lot of attention.


I am always trying to experiment, be it with materials, narratives or styles and I feel that is something that is evident in the work I'm creating.
